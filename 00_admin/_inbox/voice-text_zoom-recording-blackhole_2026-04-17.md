# Zoom Recording with Blackhole

## Steps

1. **Install BlackHole** Download BlackHole from Existential Audio's website
(existential.audio) — the 2-channel version.

2. **Create a Multi-Output Device** Open Audio MIDI Setup (find it via Spotlight)
Click the + button at the bottom left and choose Create Multi-Output Device
Check both your regular speakers/headphones and BlackHole 2ch in the list
This lets you hear audio and route it to BlackHole simultaneously

3. **Set the Multi-Output Device as Your System Output** In System Settings →
Sound → Output, select the Multi-Output Device you just created
You'll now be routing all system audio through both your speakers and BlackHole

4. **Open Your Recording App** Launch QuickTime Player or Audacity.
In QuickTime, go to File → New Audio Recording.
Click the dropdown arrow next to the record button and select BlackHole 2ch as
the input source.

Open Audacity and in the toolbar at the top, find the Audio Setup or input
device dropdown. Set the input to BlackHole 2ch. Set the output to your
Multi-Output Device (so you can still monitor the audio while recording).
Hit the red Record button before or after joining Zoom.
You'll see the waveform moving as audio comes in — that's your confirmation it's
working

After the call, Audacity gives you some handy cleanup options worth knowing:
Noise Reduction (Effects menu) — good for removing background hiss
Normalize — evens out the volume level
Export via File → Export Audio — choose MP3 or WAV depending on what you need

5. **Join the Zoom Call and Record**

Hit Record in QuickTime before or after joining Zoom
The Zoom audio will be captured through BlackHole
Your mic will still work normally in Zoom — this only captures what you hear

6. Stop and Save

When the call ends, stop the recording in QuickTime
Go to File → Save and export as an audio file (QuickTime saves as .m4a by default; Audacity gives you more format options)

A few tips.

The Multi-Output Device may disable your mic input — use a separate USB mic or your phone as a backup if needed
Test the setup before the meeting so you're not troubleshooting live
Check your organization's or jurisdiction's rules on recording calls you don't host — consent laws vary
